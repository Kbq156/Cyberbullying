About

What Is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of online technologies to harass, threaten or embarrass another person. This type of bullying can occur in a multitude of different ways, on a variety of different online platforms.

Why Is It A Problem?

With the ongoing growth of social media and its capabilities, people all over the world are able to exploit these capabilities to harm and harass others. This is especially true amongst children and teens who are using the internet as a means of spreading hate towards whoever they target. Because this kind of harassment occurs in an online space, it is harder to identify, detect and even stop altogether. As of now, so many people all over the world are interconnected through the use of their mobile devices and computers on the internet/social media. An individuals private information and even livelihood can be found through a few clicks and taps. This can be dangerous as ones reputation can be ruined by just a single persons words or pictures posted online publicly for anyone at anytime to see. In some occassions cyberbullying can transfrom into unlawful and even criminal activity as well.

Risk Factors

For many years now, bullying has been known to increase the risk of depression, anxiety and even suicide-related behaviors amongst victims. Cyberbullying is no exception and has also become notoriously known for posing these same risks to the people exposed to it. There are even some states that have passed laws to prosecute individuals who bully for criminal harrassment; this also includes children. Below you will find some examples as to how cyberbullying can transform into criminal activity that has even ended fatally for many of its victims.

Examples

Where Can It Happen?

Some places where cyberbullying can occur includes:

  • Social Media, such as Facebook, TikTok, Instagram and Snapchat
  • Text messaging and messaging applications on mobile or tablet devices
  • Online forums, chat rooms and message boards such as Reddit
  • Emails, such as Gmail, Yahoo Mail and Outlook
  • Online Gaming Communities

Viral Tactical Examples

Because cyberbullying can happen in a variety of different ways, by providing some real life scenarios a deeper understanding of the tactics employed by cyberbullies can be shown.

Here are some real-life experiences of cyberbullying that have occured:

  • Spreading Lies and False Accusations

    A group of students get in trouble at school for smoking weed. They accuse an innocent girl who knew nothing about it for reporting them to the school officals. They started texting her hateful and derogatory messages day and night along with posting harmful messages about her on social media. The posts spreads amongst the other students in the school causing more targeted hate towards the girl. This ultimately leads her to shut down her social media accounts and changing her phone number. Unfortunately, the girl continued to be bullied at school.

  • Encouraging Self-Harm or Suicide

    A boy at school with a mental disability was tormented by his peers via text messaging. They wrote him messages telling him to "kill himself" and that he's "better off being dead". This lead to the boy staying out of school to avoid being bullied more.

  • Leaking/Spreading "Nudes" Nude Photos

    A girl sends nude photos of herself to her boyfriend. She ends up breaking up with him later on. Out of anger, he decides to share these photos with his friends and leaks them online through social media for everyone to see. Everyone starts calling her derogatory names like "slut" and "whore". Because of this, her reputation has been tainted and she is not treated with the respect she deserves.

  • Doxing

    A live streamer has a take on a specific subject matter. One of his viewers disagrees with his opinion and expresses it to the streamer. The streamer whom does not appreciate this, decides to find and expose the viewers private information online for all of his other viewers to see. Another one of the streamers viewers decides to call the police and has the individuals house swatted in real life. This ended with the person being shot and killed in his own home.

These are just a handful of real-world examples as to why cyberbullying and be extremely harmful and even dangerous to the individuals experiencing it.

Help

What Can Be Done?

Now that we understand what cyberbullying is and the different ways it can happen, what can we do about it? Although it can be difficult to respond to cyberbullying because it occurs in a digital space, there are a number of steps that can be taken to help or even stop it from happening.

Here are some vital steps to take once you have identified a case of cyberbullying:

  1. Take Action Immediately
    • Do not respond to or forward cyberbullying messaging.
    • Keep evidence of cyberbullying such as saving screenshots, emails or text messages. Make sure to record the exact date and time the events occured. This evidence can be used against the perpatrator later on.
    • Block the guilty party or parties to prevent further harrassment.
  2. Report to Online Services

    Often times you will find that platforms have a strict zero-tolerance policy against cyberbullying. This can be used to further prevent/stop cyberbullying from happening.

    • Review the platforms ToS (Terms of Service), conditions or rights. There you can find the type of content that the service provider deems inapropriate and will punish all who violate them.
    • Search different ways to block users or change settings within a platform to prevent any further contact from the perpatrator.
    • Report the cyberbullying on the platform it was committed on so that proper action can be taken onto those who abused the terms of service.
  3. Report to Law Enforcement

    As stated in the Examples section of this site, cyberbullying can cross into real criminal activity and therefore should be reported to law enforcement officials.

    • Any threats of violence or threats on someones life is a crime and should be reported to the law.
    • Anything pertaining to child pornography and sexually explict messages and photos should also be reported and condemned by the law.
    • The unathorized spread of photos or videos of someone in their private space where they would expect to be private is illegal and should also be reported.
    • Any form of hate crimes and stalking can also be reported to law enforcement as they are considered to be criminal offenses.

Helping A Victim

As stated in the About section, victims of cyberbullying often face some form of fear, anxiety and depression. They often try to isolate themselves from others and avoid any form of social entanglements. Oftentimes the long-term effects of this can lead to clinical depression and even thoughts of self-harm/suicide amongst these victims.

If you know someone who is a victim of cyberbullying, don't be afraid of lending a helping hand. Bullying has always been tough to deal with for many people and now with the existence/growth of the Internet, it has spread itself to a digital layer as well making it difficult to escape from. However, having a friend or friends has been proven to promote positive mental health and well-being especially amongst those whom face these issues.

If you believe that a target of cyberbullying is in need of professional support, try to direct or alert the proper people to them. Sometimes this kind of help is necessary in order to truly help a victim.

If you know someone who is suicidal or has had thoughts of suicide/harming themselves, call the national suicide hotline at the number "988".
